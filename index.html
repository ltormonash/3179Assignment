WORLDWIDE

Mortality v Ownership

Crime Index

The trend suggests that there is a strong correlation between high gun ownership rates and deaths to violent crime . The United States is an extreme outlier in both regards. No other country accepts firearms to this extent. The presence of these weapons increases the potential deadliness of acts of violent crime.

However, violent crime index of the United States is comparable to other developed countries . It is only slightly above the worldwide average. This means that their gun violence issues do not stem from the presence of more dangerous individuals in society, but rather the accessibility and absurdly high acceptance of firearms.

UNITED STATES

Mass Shooting Events (1960-2022)

States such as California and New York with low firearm ownership experience a large proportion of the mass shooting events. Meanwhile, Northwestern states such as Montana and Wyoming have not experienced mass shootings during this period although having the highest firearm ownership percentage. A reason for this is stricter regulations for who can carry firearms in public. This makes it less accessible for the firearm to be used for violence.

FIREARM ACCESSIBILITY

Type of Firearm v Legality of Purchase

Handguns are the most popular weapon type in these shootings. Overall and across all weapon types, near half are sourced illegally . An illegal underground market is a symptom of extreme abundance of firearms (as displayed in previous graphs). When they are too prevalent in society, it is too hard to control who has access to them.

BACKGROUND ANALYSIS

Motivation of Shooters Ranking

Existing Conditions

The prevalence of social media in todays age provides these individuals with a platform to broadcast themselves. This has seen fame seeking quickly become the most common motivator, with factors such as relationship issues fading over time.

Only 5% of shooters did not have an existing mental condition. People with severe mental illnesses have an increased risk of violence compared to the general population. The ease of accessing firearms in the USA makes large scale violence an option for these people.

Criminal History

32% of Shooters were known to Police or FBI as being threats to the safety of the community before their violent act. This reveals that a significant proportion of mass shootings were preventable , but the police force failed to reprimand the individuals efficiently.
